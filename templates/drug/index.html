In the later 1990s, pharaceutical companies convinced the medical community that patients would not get addicted to opioid pain relivers. However, these medications were highly addictive and continue to be a major issue today. Around 50,000 people in the United States died from opioid-involved overdoses. This is a serious national crisis. The misuse of opioid perscription drugs alone have a total economic burden of $78.5 billion a year. These costs include addiction treatment, criminal involvement, and the cost of healthcare. From 21 to 29 percent of patients misuse opioids that were prescribed for chronic pain. Around 80 percent of people who use heroine misused perscripted opioids first. This issue continues to grow more serious. Below is a chart of the increase of deaths involving opioids since 1999. {% comment %} Risk Factors for Opioid Addiction

Common factors that increase the risk of opioid addition are:

  • History of substance abuse
  • Depression and other mental health issues
  • Childhood abuse and neglect
  • Risk-taking and thrill seeking behavior
  • Unemployment and poverty
